# CRITIQUE

## recommendedSpine
The game is P1's skeleton wearing P3's organs and P2's muscles: a persistent-within-expedition descent through a depth graph of fully simulated levels, where every lock, weapon, and potion is made of cells. Structure comes from Proposal 1 — the depth stack, descent wells, snapshots, fire-lit waystones, region-graph placement brain, material-colored map, and the merged Refuge between levels. Substance comes from Proposal 3 — the data-driven reaction matrix and hardness table as the substrate, the flask as the universal verb (siphon, carry, pour, throw, drink), cauldron brewing with Grimoire knowledge as the persistent metaprogression, and a curated ~10 new materials giving each biome a chemical identity. Combat comes from Proposal 2 — the cell-sampled status system as the keystone (built once, shared with potions), grid-reading/grid-writing enemies, the pick/pogo safety floor, and the wand-frame cast compiler in its restrained form (~14 cards at launch, depth-1 triggers, clamps in the compiler), with P3's flask-fed trail mod absorbed as an Infuser card. The contradiction between metroidvania and roguelite dissolves in the expedition model: your scars, shortcuts, and lit waystones persist within a descent; reseeding is a choice you make at the surface, never a punishment death imposes. Death is a walk back, not a reset — the web-friendly, exploration-first answer the brief demands.

Shipping order interleaves the three proposals instead of running them in parallel, each wave shippable: Wave A 'Bottles & Reactions' (reaction matrix port with golden-image tests, hardness table, flask system, acid->gold nerf, seeded RNG in the already-ported CaveGenerator.ts, plus the missing movement-feel sprint — coyote time, wall-kick, levitation curves) ships into the existing arena and immediately doubles its playability. Wave B 'The Descent' (depth graph, wells, snapshots with autosave-on-blur, waystones, minimap, placed enemy populations, refuge shell with cauldron and shop) converts the arena into a game. Wave C 'Living Walls, Living Targets' (region graph extraction, secret rooms with hollow-wall tells, status engine, first 5 new enemies, brewing online with 5 potions and the Grimoire) makes exploration and combat read the same grid. Wave D 'Wandsmith' (frames, cast compiler, 14 cards including legacy spells, bench UI, card economy, Codex merged with the card collection). Wave E 'Locks Made of Physics' (biome verb kits, sensor framework with fail-open, three puzzle archetypes — sand scale, burning seals, sluice — and the Kiln Colossus as the first boss). Branches, the Gilded Vault, remaining puzzle archetypes, a second boss, and additional cards are post-spine content drips on proven systems. Throughout: one frame-budget ledger with automated worst-case scenes as a release gate, telemetry counters from Wave A, and the one commandment enforced in every review — if the grid can't explain it, it doesn't ship.

## bestIdeas
- P1: Depth Stack World Graph + descent wells + persistence snapshots — the structural spine. One live 1600x1064 World at a time (zero added sim cost), RLE snapshots so the world remembers your scars within an expedition, reseed across expeditions. This single move converts the arena into a journey and is what the brief means by 'a better map/level system'.
- P1: Region Graph Extraction (flood-fill regions, adjacency with wall thickness, main path, articulation points) — the smartest piece of tech in all three proposals. It is the placement brain that makes secrets, puzzles, waystones, shrines, and boss arenas placed-with-intent instead of random scatter, and it grounds gating in what the sim actually lets players breach.
- P1+P3 convergent: the Flask (P1 'Material Flask', P3 'Flask System' — independently invented, which is the strongest possible signal). Siphon real cells, carry them, pour/throw them. It is the master key uniting puzzles, combat, brewing, and economy. Ship P3's richer version (3 slots, drink, glass-shard shatter) with P1's 600-cell capacity framing.
- P3: Data-driven reaction matrix + HARDNESS table — the engineering substrate. With ~10+ new materials coming from every direction, interactions must become data entries, not scattered if-chains; HARDNESS turns dig-tier gating into a one-table Metroidvania key system and deletes the METAL special-cases.
- P2: Sim-driven status system (WET/OILED/BURNING/FROZEN/ELECTRIFIED sampled from the cells touching your body, statuses write cells back) — the keystone that fuses combat with the sand sim with zero bookkeeping desync. Every other combat idea composes with this.
- P2: Wand frames + deterministic no-shuffle cast compiler with depth-1 trigger cap — the right 'more weapons' answer at web scale. The restraint decisions (readable left-to-right, wait-on-mana, x4 damage clamp enforced in the compiler) are load-bearing and correct.
- P2: Enemy roster that reads and writes the grid (Spark Eel charging its liquid body, Stone Maw leaving persistent tunnels, Powder Mage throwing the level at you) plus the Alchemist's Pick / pogo kick as the zero-mana safety floor that makes wand experimentation safe.
- P3: Cauldron brewing as a literal grid histogram with heat-under-basin detection, mis-brews as content, and Grimoire recipe knowledge persisting across runs — this is the moment the title earns 'Alchemist', and it costs almost nothing beyond the matrix core.
- P1: Fire-lit waystones (bring real fire to the brazier; Flooded-biome waystones become micro-puzzles) and the steam-updraft re-ascension on cleared wells — S-effort features with enormous identity payoff.
- P1: Secret rooms that obey the sim + hollow-wall resonance tells — the cheapest pure exploration joy, and the tutorialization engine in disguise: secrets train players that materials are verbs, which is the prerequisite for every puzzle landing.
- P1: Biome verb kits + sensor-cell framework (pressure/buoy/charge-latch/burn-fuse) with the fail-open rule — mechanical biome diversity is the brief's hardest mandate, and 'sensors read raw cells so enemy blood counts as conductor' guarantees emergent solutions are always valid. The fail-open rule is the best anti-softlock design in any proposal.
- P3: Material Codex first-discovery bounties wired to reaction-matrix events — turns the interaction table itself into collectible exploration content for nearly free; merge with P2's card codex into one knowledge layer.
- P2: Kiln Colossus boss design (water-plug flood = thermal shock + stone bridges, pylon lightning stun) — a physics puzzle wearing a health bar, built entirely from existing reactions. The template for all future bosses.
- P1: Material-colored fog-of-war minimap that samples live World.types — your drained lakes and lava spills ARE the cartography; cheap, on-identity, and the map system the brief asked for.

## cuts
- P2's full 27-card / 6-frame launch set: cut to ~14 cards and 4 frames for the compiler's first ship. The compiler's value is visible with few cards; 27 cards x 6 frames x 5 statuses x 30 materials is a balance surface a solo dev cannot tune. Add cards as content drips, not as a wave.
- P2's three bosses: ship ONE (Kiln Colossus — most sim-driven, least novel tech). Cut the Hollow Choir entirely: sister anger-links, resurrection timers, and heart-melt denial is the most scripted, least falling-sand fight proposed, and triple-entity boss tuning is a known time sink. Dross Queen's live 250-cell skirt repaint is unproven render tech — if she ships later, use the sprite+small-skirt fallback from day one.
- P3's Mercury: one material carrying five special rules (super-conductor, eats gold, boils to gas, freezes to temporary metal bridges, poisons on touch) is three materials' worth of tuning hiding in one ID. Cut it; its 'temporary bridge' verb is already covered by water+lava=stone and nitrogen freezing.
- P3's Crystal charge-nova Tesla conduits and translucent Glass rendering: the nova chains are a perf cliff in crystal caves, and the per-pixel translucency branch in the hot fill loop is exactly the kind of cost the proposal itself flags — use the pre-dithered opaque palette and make Crystal just a glowing mineable solid.
- P3's separate Essence currency and Workshop hub screen: three currencies (gold, essence, material offerings) is one too many. Fold meta-progression into P1's Surface Camp (shortcut unlocks, discovered-spell pool) + the Grimoire/Codex knowledge layer. Knowledge and unlocked options ARE the metaprogression; no abstract currency needed.
- P1's Gilded Vault as an acid->gold 'money printer' celebration: directly contradicts P3's necessary economy guardrail (nerf to 3% + water-adjacent, shipped in the same wave as flasks). Keep the Vault as a secret biome but make its payoff a hoard + unique spell, with transmutation amplified only by a vault-local catalyst material, never globally.
- P1's branch biomes (Flooded alt, Fungal Gloom) and Spore Gas/Mycelium at launch: branches double the tuning surface before the trunk is proven — P1's own wave 5 placement is correct, defend it. Fungal Gloom's confined-space chain detonations are the single most player-killing tuning hazard proposed.
- P2's destructible dropped wands (acid/fire eats loot): flavor not worth the grief of a boss reward melting in the boss's own death lava. Wands are indestructible, full stop.
- P2's dry-fire pocket-sim preview in the wand bench: an entire second World instance for a tooltip. Defer indefinitely; a static cast-pattern diagram covers 90% of the need.
- P3's 'the way back seals' (bedrock ceiling) and portable cauldron: the seal contradicts the persistent-backtracking spine (see conflicts), and the portable cauldron deletes the site-selection gameplay that makes brewing interesting. Cut both.
- P3's shrine wrong-pour curses and Void Salt's mana-drain-on-touch: punishing experimentation contradicts the game's one commandment (experiment with materials). Keep overpour-is-wasted; keep Void Salt's liquid-annihilation verb; cut the punishments.
- P1's Cursed Stone AND P3's Obsidian AND P1's Frostglass as separate gate materials: collapse the gate-material zoo. One hardness ladder (P3's table) + two or three soluble gate materials max. Every gate material is a save-format ABI commitment and a spell-audit row (P1's own excavate/black-hole audit risk).
- P2's per-biome shrine trio (Anvil of Fusion / Mana Spring / Chaos Altar) as a separate placement system: merge into the single Refuge structure (see conflicts) — keep the Anvil and Mana Spring as refuge fixtures, cut the Chaos Altar gamble until the wand economy is stable.

## conflicts
- Persistent metroidvania vs roguelite runs (P1 vs P2/P3 — the headline conflict): side with P1's expedition model, because it already contains both: WITHIN an expedition the world is persistent (snapshots, scars, waystones, shortcut wells — Hollow Knight), ACROSS expeditions it reseeds (Dead Cells/Noita replayability). P2's per-run wand builds and P3's per-run brews slot cleanly into 'per-expedition'. Kill P3's ceiling-seal commitment and P3's death-converts-gold-to-essence; death is P1's waystone respawn with world intact + recoverable 15% gold stain. A web game must be forgiving about session length, and persistence is what no other Noita-like does.
- Two competing spell-mod systems (P2's cast compiler vs P3's tinker bench): they cannot coexist — same player need, same UI surface. Side with P2's compiler as the destination because 'more weapons' demands it and the frame/card grammar is the combat identity, BUT adopt P3's killer feature inside it: the flask-fed trail mod becomes an 'Infuser' modifier card whose material payload is loaded by pouring a flask into it. That merge gives P3's '80% of the emergence' inside P2's architecture instead of beside it. Ship the bench-style 3-slot version first only if the compiler slips.
- Two status frameworks (P2's cell-sampled meters vs P3's potion bitfield+timers): build ONE entity status struct, with P2's grid-is-source-of-truth rule for environmental statuses and P3's potion effects expressed as timed rewrites of entity-vs-cell rules (stoneskin writes stone, acid blood swaps splatter material) — P3's own framing makes this compatible. The non-negotiable: no status may exist that the grid can't explain.
- Acid->gold economy (P1 celebrates the printer, P3 nerfs it): P3 wins, and the nerf MUST ship in the same wave as flasks — portable acid plus the current 10% rule is an infinite-money hose. P1's Vault keeps the fantasy via local amplification only.
- Three between-level safe rooms invented independently (P1 Alchemist's Refuge, P2 shop idol + shrines, P3 Rest Stop): convergent evolution — merge into ONE Refuge prefab containing P3's cauldron, the shared shop, P2's wand bench and Anvil, P3's offering shrine, and the healing spring (P1 and P3 both invented 'drink the sim' springs). One prefab, one placement system, one snapshot story.
- Enemy direction (P1 fixed placed populations vs P2 wave-clear rebalance vs P3 ambush director): P1's gen-time placed populations win as the base — exploration needs readable, finite threats — augmented by P3's ambush triggers (vault cracks, lingering 90s). Endless waves die with the arena. P2's choice-of-3 chests retarget from wave-clears to nest-clears and vaults.
- World structure (P1's 7-level graph with branches vs P3's 6-deck linear regen): P1 wins because its snapshot persistence is the enabling tech for leave-and-return puzzles, secrets, and the map mandate; P3's decks are a strict subset. But adopt P3's depth-scaling formulas and biome material identities as the content of P1's levels.
- Who owns 'side pocket' and 'gate' placement (P2's risk list explicitly flags this): P1's region graph is the single authority — shrines, boss arenas, secrets, sensors, and set-pieces all request placements from it. Decide this interface before any content wave starts, exactly as P2 asked.
- Materials roster overlap (P1's Tar/Obsidian/Spore Gas/Mycelium/gate materials vs P3's 12): one curated merged list of ~10 new cells for the first year — Glass, Ash, Slag, Toxic Gas, Toxic Sludge, Spores, Brimstone, Obsidian, Honey, Void Salt — with P1's Tar folded into Oil-variant behavior and gate materials capped per the cuts. Every cell ID is forever (snapshot ABI, both proposals agree); append-only with version stamps from day one.

## missing
- Onboarding and the first 15 minutes: all three proposals assume players already think in material verbs. Nobody designed the teaching layer — D1 needs authored moments (a wooden seal with a brazier beside it; a sand plug over a visible treasure; one free flask with water pre-filled next to a small lava pool). The secrets system trains experimentation mid-game, but minute one is undesigned in 40+ pages of proposals.
- Input/controls budget: the merged design wants move, jump, levitate, fire, wand-swap, melee, pogo, flask cycle, siphon/pour, throw, drink, map, pins, bench, interact — roughly 15 verbs. Nobody audited this against a keyboard+mouse layout, let alone gamepad or the reality that web games get played on laptops with trackpads. A controls bible and a 'no new input without removing one' rule is needed before Wave 2 of anything.
- Movement game feel: the brief names Hollow Knight, Ori, and Dead Cells — three movement-feel games — and only P1's one-line 'wall-kick' and P2's pogo touch it. Coyote time, jump buffering, levitation response curves, hitstop, knockback tuning, and landing feedback are absent from all three. This deserves a dedicated feel sprint BEFORE content waves, because every hour of content is played through the movement.
- A combined frame-budget ledger: each proposal caps its own systems (sensor reads, flood-fill caps, trail budgets, 4-frame sampling) but nobody owns the SUM — status sampling + sensors + emitters + projectile trails + minimap stamping + dynamic lighting + 70 placed enemies + snapshot encode can each be 'negligible' and collectively blow 16ms. Need an explicit ledger (e.g., sim 6ms / entities 2.5ms / render 5ms / 2.5ms headroom), a perf HUD, and 3-4 automated worst-case scenes (spore cloud + flamethrower + 64 projectiles + minimap refresh) run on a low-end laptop as a release gate.
- Stale codebase assumptions (verified): P1 plans a 'generator port' from noita-sandbox.html:3440 and P2 plans around 'the HTML still owns spells/enemies/player' — both already done. src/world/CaveGenerator.ts (586 lines, Uint8Array ping-pong) and src/combat/Spells.ts, src/entities/Enemies.ts, src/entities/Player.ts all exist. Wave 1 estimates shrink, but the REAL unclaimed task surfaces: CaveGenerator.ts uses bare Math.random throughout (lines 52-175) — seeded mulberry32 RNG plus a determinism test harness is a discrete, owner-less work item that everything (snapshots, level regen, daily seeds) depends on.
- Mid-session save/resume for web: P1's IndexedDB snapshots cover level transitions, but web players close tabs mid-level. Nobody designed autosave-on-blur/visibilitychange into the snapshot system, tab-suspend recovery, or what 'continue' means halfway between waystones. Losing 20 minutes to a closed tab churns harder than any death.
- Failure-state escape valves beyond gates: P1's fail-open rule covers sensor gates, P3's 'excavate always works on hardness 0-1' covers entombment — but nobody covers the merged game's 'I ruined this level' states: drained the healing spring, flooded the refuge, burned the only wood, broke the cauldron mid-brew. Need a designed physics-mulligan (waystones regenerate refuge fixtures; springs slowly re-drip) and a softlock audit checklist per biome.
- Telemetry and playtest instrumentation from Wave 1: only P3 mentions telemetry, in its final wave. An emergence-heavy game cannot be tuned by feel alone — deaths by cause, puzzle solve-method distribution, secret find rates, flask material usage, and card pick rates should be counted locally from the first shipped wave.
- Material readability and accessibility: 30+ materials distinguished only by procedural palette — cyan nitrogen vs pale water vs ice, oil vs tar vs sludge — with no colorblind audit, no in-PLAY hover-identify (P3's codex hover is the seed of the answer), and no audio-cue language per material family. The sim is only a fair puzzle if players can read it.
- Audio production budget: ~10 new enemies, ~14 cards, ~10 materials, brewing, sensors, and bosses all assume 'sfxKey' exists. Procedural audio is the right call, but nobody scoped the mixing problem — ducking and voice-stealing when six chain explosions, a flamethrower, and a geyser fire simultaneously is a design task, not a detail.


# DESIGN 1

## vision
You are an alchemist-wizard descending a persistent, fully simulated underworld — a vertical stack of seven hand-shaped-by-algorithm cave levels where every wall is a question: will it burn, dissolve, flood, freeze, or conduct? The arena becomes a Dead-Cells-style depth graph of 1600x1064 worlds connected by wells; Hollow Knight supplies the persistent scars (everything you dig, burn, or flood stays that way), waystones, and the joy of re-reading a map you've marked; Noita supplies the truth that the simulation is the lock AND the key. What makes it distinct from all three references: there are no scripted doors — every gate, secret, shortcut, and puzzle is built from the existing 21-material cellular automata, so the player's tools for exploration are the same tools they fight with, and every 'puzzle' has at least two physical solutions because physics doesn't care which one you found.

## coreLoop
MINUTE-TO-MINUTE: drop into a dark pocket → scout with your wand-light → read the walls (gold flecks, crack pixels, hollow 'thunk' on projectile hit = something behind this) → choose a verb (dig sand, burn wood, pour acid, flood, freeze) to open a route → fight the pocket's inhabitants amid the mess you just made → siphon a useful material into your flask → light a fire-waystone (you must BRING fire to it) → solve the biome's signature physics lock at the chokepoint → break the floor seal and drop down the descent well. RUN-TO-RUN: each depth introduces one new material verb and offers one movement relic; death respawns you at your last lit waystone with the world's damage intact and 15% gold lost — never a reset; reaching a new depth lets you blast open its shortcut well from below, permanently linking it to the surface camp; 'New Expedition' reseeds the whole depth graph but keeps shortcut unlocks and every spell you've ever found in the starting shop pool, so each descent is faster, deeper, and starts with more options.

## features

### Depth Stack World Graph [world, M]
Replace the single arena with a graph of 7+ levels, each its own 1600x1064 World instance: Surface Camp (hub, mostly sky) → D1 Earthen Hollows → D2 Timberworks OR Flooded Caverns (player picks left/right well) → D3 Frozen Depths OR Fungal Gloom → D4 Galvanic Foundry → D5 Scorched Core → boss vault. Graph defined as data in src/config/worldgraph.ts: { id, biome, depth, exits: [{wellX, targetLevelId, sealMaterial}] }. Each level seeded by mulberry32(expeditionSeed ^ levelId) replacing the generator's bare Math.random so any level regenerates identically. WaveDirector becomes per-level: fixed enemy population placed at gen time (45-70 per level scaled by depth) instead of endless waves; gold bounties unchanged.
SIM: Only the active level's World exists in the sim loop — zero added per-tick cost. The existing simBounds camera-window logic is untouched. Enemy placement uses the generator's region data so spawns are inside open pockets, not walls.

### Descent Wells & Streaming Transitions [world, L]
Each level's floor band (currently an open strip at HEIGHT-52) becomes a Bedrock seal pierced by 1-2 capped wells: 28-cell-wide vertical shafts plugged with 14 rows of the biome's gate material (Stone at D1, Frostglass at D3, etc.). Break the plug → fall through → 600ms fade curtain → snapshot old level, generate/restore next, spawn in the top chamber. Generation must move off JS array-of-arrays: port the CA to two ping-pong Uint8Arrays (1600x1064 = 1.7MB each) and run the 5 smoothing passes + artery carving in a chunked coroutine, 8 rows-bands per rAF, total budget <700ms hidden behind the curtain (or a Web Worker if measured >700ms). Re-ascending a cleared level's well grants a steam updraft (emitter column, 3 steam cells/tick) that lifts the player at 1.5x levitation speed for free.
SIM: The well plug is real cells — you can also melt/dissolve it if you have the right material, and debris from breaking it rains into the level below on arrival (spawn the broken cells falling above the player). The updraft is literally Steam cells driving the existing gas sim, so it also pushes smoke, embers, and enemies.

### Persistence Snapshots (the world remembers) [world, M]
On leaving a level: RLE-encode World.types (cave worlds compress to ~120-400KB from 1.7MB raw) + sparse lists for life/charge nonzero cells + stain decals + entity roster (type, x, y, hp — dead stay dead) + picked-up loot flags. Colors are NOT stored: regenerate deterministically via colorFor(type, hash2(x,y)) on restore, then re-apply the stain list. Keep the 3 most recent snapshots in RAM (~1.2MB), spill the rest to IndexedDB keyed by expeditionSeed+levelId. Restore is a single decode pass + 30-tick settle simulation run over the spawn chamber's sim window before fade-in. Cell enum values are already documented stable — append-only from here, version-stamp every snapshot.
SIM: Snapshots capture the sim state exactly as the player left it: drained lakes stay drained, burned scaffolds stay ash, the stone bridge you made by pouring water on lava is still there. Because off-window cells are already frozen by simBounds, snapshot-on-exit is lossless by construction.

### Fog-of-War Minimap & Full Map Overlay [exploration, M]
Per level: a 200x133 Uint8Array explored mask (1:8 downsample, 26.6KB — stored in the snapshot). Every 8 frames, stamp a radius-6 mask-cell disc (48 world cells) around the player, widened to radius 9 where the existing light field is bright. Corner minimap: 200x133 DataTexture showing downsampled dominant material color per explored mask cell, refreshed at 4Hz from the dirty-stamped region only. M key: full-screen overlay at 4x scale with a depth-stack sidebar (biome names, explored %, waystone/well/refuge icons, player-placed pins — 8 pin glyphs, place with right-click). Cartographer Lectern in each level: interacting back-fills the structural outline (walls vs open from the generator's region data) for the whole level in a dimmer shade, Hollow-Knight style — you still must walk it to see materials.
SIM: The minimap renders MATERIAL colors, not abstract rooms — a cyan smear means nitrogen lake, orange means lava, and because it samples live World.types, the map updates when you permanently drain or flood an area. Hazards you created are cartography.

### Region Graph Extraction (the generator grows a brain) [world, M]
After CA passes + artery carving, downsample the work buffer 1:4 (400x266) and flood-fill label open regions (typical level: 30-80 regions). Build adjacency: for each boundary cell pair, record the minimum wall thickness between neighboring regions (scan 8 directions up to 8 downsampled cells). Compute the BFS main path spawn→well and articulation regions (cut vertices) on the graph. Output: regions[] {area, centroid, onMainPath, isPocket}, edges[] {a, b, minWallThickness, midpoint}, chokepoints[]. This ~30ms pass (typed arrays, single flood fill) is the placement substrate for secrets, puzzle gates, waystones, enemy nests, and loot — everything stops being random scatter and starts being placed with intent.
SIM: Pure analysis of the same boolean field the CA produces — zero runtime cost. Wall-thickness data directly drives which walls are 'breachable in practice' (a 5-cell skin yields to one bomb; a 30-cell mass needs the excavate ray), so gating decisions are grounded in what the sim will actually let players do.

### Secret Rooms That Obey the Sim [exploration, M]
Per level, stamp 5-8 secrets: pick wall masses ≥24 cells thick adjacent to main-path regions, carve an enclosed ellipse (rx 18-30, ry 12-20) leaving a 4-7 cell skin, and re-skin that wall in a breach material keyed to biome: Wood (burn it), packed Sand (dig or flood it out), Ice (melt), gold-flecked Stone (3% Gold cells as the tell, excavate ray), Cursed Stone (acid only). Also adopt natural CA pockets (unreachable regions, area 600-4000) as free secrets. Tells: 1-2 crack pixels of off-palette color, a faint particle drip every ~3s, and the hollow-thunk audio cue (below). Contents: 40% gold cache (200-600), 25% flask/material cache, 15% spell or relic, 10% lore tablet, 10% slime nest with doubled loot. Guarantee exactly 1 relic-bearing secret per level. Secrets found feed the map screen's per-level percentage.
SIM: There is no 'secret wall' flag — the wall IS the secret, made of cells that respond to fire/acid/water/digging exactly like that material does everywhere else. Players discover them by playing with physics (a stray flamethrower sweep opens a wooden seam), which trains experimentation everywhere.

### Hollow-Wall Tells (feel layer for secrets) [feel, S]
When a projectile or dig ray hits a wall cell, raycast 10 cells along the impact normal; if open space lies behind ≤8 cells of solid, play a lower-pitched resonant impact (existing procedural AudioEngine: drop base freq ~30%, add 120ms ring) and emit 2-3 dust particles from the far side's micro-cracks. Costs one 10-cell scan per impact — negligible. Same scan powers a late-game 'Dowsing' boon that tints thin walls +6% brightness within 60 cells.
SIM: Reads World.types directly along the ray; works on every thin wall in the game, natural or stamped, so CA-generated coincidental pockets become discoverable secrets for free.

### Biome Identity Through Materials & Hazards [world, L]
Extend BiomeDef beyond palette+counts to a verb kit: signature placed materials, hazard emitters, gate material, and puzzle archetype. D1 Earthen: sand chimneys (vertical 8x60 sand columns that collapse when undercut), water pockets; verb = dig/flood. D2 Timberworks: wood lattice scaffolds, oil channels along surfaces, gunpowder kegs (12x10 stamps); verb = controlled burning; new cell TAR (flammable liquid, viscosity 3x oil, heavy black smoke). D2-alt Flooded: 62% flood retained, geyser emitter cells (eject 4 water cells/tick for 90 ticks, 600-tick cooldown), air-pocket management (drowning meter 12s, steam creates breathable bubbles); verb = drain/route. D3 Frozen: nitrogen lakes, brittle ice shelves (break under entity weight after 45 ticks of standing); verb = freeze/shatter. D3-alt Fungal Gloom: new cells SPORE GAS (flammable, rises slow, detonates like 0.5x gunpowder) and MYCELIUM (vine variant that regrows 1 cell/40 ticks unless burned to its root cell); lighting radius halved; verb = vent or ignite gas. D4 Foundry: metal beam lattices, electrified water channels (permanent charge sources), lava boilers; verb = conduct. D5 Scorched: lava seas 80-150 cells wide, gunpowder veins in walls, obsidian (new cell: blast-proof, acid-soluble); verb = make stone (water+lava=stone already exists — it becomes the central traversal tool). 5 new cells total, all using existing element behavior templates.
SIM: Every biome difference is a sim difference: Frozen's hazard is the nitrogen interaction that already exists, Foundry runs on the existing charge array, Scorched is built around the existing water+lava=stone rule. Palette becomes the SECOND thing you notice about a biome.

### Sensor Cell Framework (physics-readable switches) [world, M]
A sparse list of sensor zones (not a grid sweep): each sensor is {type, rect, threshold, holdTicks, latched, targetGateId} checked every 4th sim tick by counting matching cells in its rect (rects are 100-300 cells; with ≤6 sensors per level that's <2k cell reads per check — trivial). Four sensor types: PRESSURE (count powder/solid cells in a 9x14 hopper ≥90 for 60 ticks), BUOY (count Water cells in a 12x8 basin ≥60 for 90 ticks), CHARGE LATCH (any cell in rect has charge>0 for 60 cumulative ticks — uses the existing electrical propagation), BURN FUSE (all N registered wood-seal clusters reduced below 20% of original cells). Gates are 6-cell-thick Metal Door cell spans that retract 1 row per 6 ticks when triggered (cells converted to Empty with rising dust particles). Fail-open rule: if a sensor's mechanism cells are >80% destroyed, the gate groans and opens 30s later — physics chaos can never hard-lock progression.
SIM: Sensors read raw World.types/charge — they cannot tell flask-poured water from diverted rainfall from enemy blood (blood conducts!), so emergent solutions are automatically valid. Gate retraction is cell mutation, so a half-open gate is real cover and real material.

### Per-Biome Puzzle Stamps at Chokepoints [exploration, XL]
The generator places 1 mandatory lock at an articulation region on the main path + 1 optional treasure lock per level, stamped and then validated against the region graph (retry up to 3 seeds on failure). Archetypes: (1) SAND SCALE [Earthen]: pressure hopper under a sealed gate; a sand chimney holding 400-700 sand cells sits 30-80 cells away — undercut it and herd the sand, or fill the hopper with anything heavy. (2) BURNING SEALS [Timberworks]: gate fused to 3 wood seals 40-80 cells apart, each baffled from direct flame; a generated oil channel (8-cell-wide groove tracing region boundaries) links them to one ignition inlet — light the right end, watch fire propagate. Risk: a misfire ignites the keg room. (3) SLUICE [Flooded]: upper reservoir stamped with 1500-3000 water cells, buoy basin below, 3 candidate channels plugged by sand/wood/stone — open the right plugs to route water; validation guarantees reservoir ≥1.5x basin requirement. (4) FREEZE BRIDGE [Frozen]: 70-110 cell nitrogen lake gap; a ceiling drip emitter (2 water cells/30 ticks) can be diverted with carved grooves so falling water freezes into a walkable crust; or levitate it with a big budget, or stone-cap it if you brought water+lava. (5) LIVE CIRCUIT [Foundry]: two metal contact pads 30-60 cells apart bridged only by conductor — route water from a guaranteed reservoir within 80 cells, or pour acid (it conducts but eats the pads in ~8s — a timed solve), or lure a bleeding enemy across.
SIM: Every archetype is assembled purely from existing cell interactions (sand gravity, oil fire propagation, water flow, nitrogen freezing, charge conduction). The stamps only ARRANGE materials; the sim IS the puzzle logic, which is why each lock has 2+ legitimate solutions without any extra code.

### Material Flask (siphon the world, pour it elsewhere) [alchemy, M]
One equippable flask (later upgradable to 3 slots): hold the siphon key to vacuum up to 600 cells of any liquid or powder within a radius-8 cone (drains ~40 cells/tick); throw (arcs as a particle, shatters on impact spawning the stored cells) or pour (10 cells/tick stream). HUD shows fill level and material color. This is the master key that makes every puzzle and gate portable: carry water to the lava sea to cast stone bridges, carry lava up to melt Frostglass, carry sand to a pressure hopper, carry acid to a Cursed Stone seal, carry nitrogen to flash-freeze a waterfall.
SIM: Siphoning deletes real cells; pouring spawns real cells via the existing brush/spawnCircle path — nothing is abstracted into an inventory icon except the count. Stored material keeps its identity, so a flask of blood is a portable conductor and a flask of gunpowder is a bomb you assemble on site.

### Fire-Lit Waystones (checkpoints you must earn) [progression, S]
2 waystones per level, placed by the region graph at main-path regions ~33% and ~66% of the way to the well (centroid-snapped to a floor surface). A waystone is a 7x12 stone brazier stamp that activates only when Fire cells touch its bowl for 30 ticks — bring flamethrower, a burning twig of wood, an ember, or splash oil and spark it. Lit waystones: set respawn, refill HP to 100%, refill flasks, persist forever in the snapshot. Death: respawn at last lit waystone, world damage intact, enemies in that region respawn at 60% density, lose 15% carried gold (recoverable Ori-style from a gold-stain splat where you died).
SIM: Activation is a sensor-cell check for Fire in the bowl rect — meaning Flooded Caverns waystones are a genuine micro-puzzle (keep fire alive near water: carry oil, make an air pocket), and a forest-fire accident can light one for you from across the room.

### Alchemist's Refuge (between-level safe rooms) [progression, L]
Noita's Holy Mountain, alchemized: every descent well lands in a hand-stamped 220x140 prefab refuge (extends the existing fortress.ts stamp system) before the next level proper. Contents: healing spring (water pool that heals 2 HP per 10 cells absorbed — yes, you drink the sim), shop with 4 rotating items priced 80-450 gold (spells, flask upgrades, relics from the pool), 1-of-3 boon shrine (run modifiers: +0.6s levitation, +15% spell damage, dowsing tint, etc.), Cartographer Lectern for the level below. Leaving collapses the entrance behind you (ceiling cells converted to falling sand+stone) unless the level's shortcut well has been opened.
SIM: The refuge is built from cells like everything else — greedy players can excavate the shop's gold inlay (the shopkeeper golem turns hostile, elite stats 3x golem), and the healing spring can be flask-siphoned and carried out as a 600-cell health potion.

### Soft-Gated Movement Relics [progression, M]
Levitation gets a visible budget bar: base 1.6s of thrust, +0.6s per Zephyr Sac relic (4 in the world: 1 shop, 2 secrets, 1 boss), cap 4.0s, recharges in 1.2s grounded. New relics gating depth SOFTLY: AQUALUNG (underwater time 12s→45s; Flooded is brutal-but-possible without it), EMBER CLOAK (lava graze 8 HP/s→2 HP/s; Scorched expects it OR mass stone-bridging), VINE GRAPPLE spell (fires a seed that grows a real Vines line up to 70 cells toward the anchor point at 4 cells/tick — climbable, flammable, cuttable), WALL-KICK (free, in Wave 1 — pure platforming feel). Every gate has a sim workaround: no relic is ever a hard key, it just changes the cost of a route from 'engineering project' to 'walk through'.
SIM: Vine Grapple writes actual Vines cells into the world — enemies path on it, fire eats it, it persists in the snapshot as a permanent ladder you built. Aqualung and Ember Cloak modify damage rates from real cell contact, not collision flags, so steam pockets and stone caps remain superior player-made answers.

### Shortcut Wells & Surface Camp [progression, M]
Each level (D2+) hides one sealed return shaft: a 20-cell-wide Bedrock-lined bore rising to the level above, plugged at its BOTTOM with a 16-row stone seal breakable only from below (one-way unlock, Hollow Knight door logic in cell form). Opening it chains upward: when all seals between a depth and the surface are open, the Surface Camp gains a labeled drop-shaft straight to that refuge (~8 second freefall with steam brakes). Camp is a persistent mini-level: shop, trophy wall (relics found), expedition board (reseed button + seed entry field + last-3-expedition stats). Shortcut unlocks persist ACROSS expeditions in the meta-save (IndexedDB), Dead Cells rune-style.
SIM: Seals are cells; the 'one-way' is geometry, not script — you physically can't reach the seal from above because the bore is capped by 40 cells of Bedrock there. The freefall drop interacts with everything: you can drop through the shaft while it rains debris you knocked loose last visit.

### Branch & Secret Biomes [exploration, L]
Wire the alternates into the graph: D2 and D3 each offer two wells (left/right) into sibling biomes (Timberworks/Flooded, Frozen/Fungal), both converging at the next refuge — a Dead Cells diamond, so one expedition sees ~5 of 7 trunk biomes and replays differ. Plus 1 secret biome per expedition, the GILDED VAULT: a 1600x500 half-height level reachable only through a secret well (hidden behind a Cursed Stone skin in a random D2-D4 level, found via dowsing/tells). Vault identity: gold-veined stone everywhere (the acid→gold transmutation rule makes acid flasks a money printer here), patrolled by 2 elite golems, contains a guaranteed relic + a unique spell.
SIM: Branch choice is made by which floor seal you break — committing physically, since the unbroken well stays sealed in the snapshot until you return. The Vault is the alchemy fantasy realized through an existing rule (acid transmutes to gold) given a whole level to be broken in.

## waves

### Wave 1 — The Descent (one arena becomes seven)
- Port generateCaves() from noita-sandbox.html:3440 into src/world/CaveGenerator.ts on Uint8Array ping-pong buffers with chunked/coroutine execution (<700ms budget)
- Seeded RNG (mulberry32 per expeditionSeed^levelId) replacing Math.random in generation
- Depth Stack World Graph data file + per-level enemy population replacing endless waves
- Descent wells: Bedrock floor seal + breakable plug + fade transition + spawn-at-top
- Persistence snapshots (RLE types + sparse life/charge + entity roster) for the 3 most recent levels in RAM
- Fire-lit Waystone checkpoints (2 per level) with the Fire-sensor activation
- Corner fog-of-war minimap (200x133 mask, material-colored)
- Wall-kick movement polish + steam updraft on cleared wells for re-ascension
WHY: Highest fun-per-effort by a mile: the exact same sim, enemies, and spells become a journey instead of a survival pen the moment there is a 'down'. Everything later (secrets, puzzles, shortcuts, branches) hangs off the level graph, the seeded generator, and snapshots — and this wave is fully shippable as 'Noita-like descent mode' with five existing biomes reskinned by depth order. The generator port is also the single riskiest tech item, so it goes first where there's schedule room to absorb surprises.

### Wave 2 — Walls That Lie (secrets and the map that finds them)
- Region Graph Extraction pass (flood-fill labels, adjacency with wall thickness, main path, articulation points) — the placement brain
- Secret room stamping (5-8 per level) with biome-keyed breach skins and the contents table
- Hollow-wall tells: resonant impact audio + crack pixels + far-side dust
- First gating cells: Bedrock (level frame), Cursed Stone (acid-soluble), Frostglass (heat-soluble)
- Full map overlay (M) with depth sidebar, pins, explored %, and the Cartographer Lectern outline-fill
- Waystone/well/refuge icons on the map; secrets counted per level
WHY: Second because secrets are the cheapest pure-exploration joy multiplier once levels exist, and they need zero new sim code — only placement intelligence. The region graph built here is load-bearing infrastructure for Wave 3's puzzle stamps and Wave 4's waystone/shortcut placement, so paying for it now de-risks both. Ships as 'the update where the walls started keeping secrets'.

### Wave 3 — Locks Made of Physics (biomes get verbs)
- Sensor Cell Framework (pressure / buoy / charge-latch / burn-fuse + retracting Metal Door gates + fail-open rule)
- Five puzzle stamp archetypes placed at articulation chokepoints with region-graph validation and 3-seed retry
- Material Flask (siphon 600 cells / throw / pour) — the portable puzzle key
- Biome identity deep pass: sand chimneys, oil channel networks, geysers + drowning meter, brittle ice, electrified channels, lava seas; new cells Tar and Obsidian
- Biome-specific hazard emitters wired into BiomeDef as data
WHY: Needs Wave 2's region graph and delivers the brief's hardest mandate: biomes that differ MECHANICALLY, not cosmetically. Ordered after secrets because puzzles are dramatically better once players have already been trained (by secrets) to treat materials as verbs. The Material Flask lands here because it converts every puzzle from local riddle to logistics sandbox — the single highest-leverage item in the design.

### Wave 4 — The Long Way Back (the progression spine)
- Alchemist's Refuge prefab between every depth (healing spring, shop, boon shrine, lectern, collapsing exit)
- Movement relics: Zephyr Sacs / Aqualung / Ember Cloak / Vine Grapple spell
- Shortcut wells with bottom-side seals + Surface Camp hub with unlocked drop-shafts
- IndexedDB meta-save: snapshots spill, shortcut unlocks, discovered-spell pool, expedition board with reseed + seed entry
- Gold-stain death recovery + respawn density tuning
WHY: Progression systems only earn their keep once there is enough world to traverse, gold to spend, and reasons to return — all delivered by Waves 1-3. This wave converts a good descent into a game people finish and restart: relics give build identity, refuges give pacing valleys between sim chaos, and shortcuts erase the backtracking tax right when total depth starts to make it sting.

### Wave 5 — Branches and Vaults (content multiplication)
- Branch wiring: dual wells at D2/D3 into sibling biomes (Flooded alt, Fungal Gloom with Spore Gas + Mycelium cells)
- Gilded Vault secret biome with acid-to-gold economy and elite golems
- Boss vault at D5: stone-bridging set piece across a 150-cell lava ocean + boss reusing golem AI scaled 4x with material attacks
- Expedition meta polish: NG+ depth modifiers (denser hazards, remixed gate materials), last-3-runs stats board
WHY: Last because it is pure content multiplication on proven systems — every biome, sensor, and stamp pattern already exists, so alternates are cheap data + one or two new cells each. Shipping it earlier would double content surface while the underlying topology and persistence were still moving targets. This is the wave that makes expedition #2 through #10 feel different, which only matters after waves 1-4 make expedition #1 great.

## risks
- Generator port is a prerequisite, not an extension: the design targets src/world/CaveGenerator.ts but generateCaves() currently exists only inside noita-sandbox.html (line 3440) on JS arrays-of-arrays — the 5 CA passes over 1.7M cells will blow the 700ms transition budget unless rewritten on Uint8Arrays (and possibly a Worker). Measure this in Wave 1 before anything depends on transition speed.
- RLE snapshot size degrades exactly where the game is most fun: heavily mixed liquid/debris regions RLE poorly. Mitigate with a hard cap (3 in-RAM snapshots, IndexedDB spill) and a worst-case fallback of raw-types storage (1.7MB) — never let save size dictate how messy players may make the world.
- Soft gates can be silently deleted by the excavate ray and black hole: audit every gate/seal material against every spell each wave; Bedrock and Metal Door must be explicitly excluded from dig/black-hole destruction lists or the entire lock layer evaporates. Conversely, never extend that immunity to puzzle MECHANISMS — destroying those triggers the 30s fail-open, not a softlock.
- Puzzle stamps vs. CA terrain collisions: a stamped sluice can generate with its reservoir already breached by a cave pocket. The region-graph validation + 3-seed retry handles most cases, but budget a repair pass (re-seal stamp perimeters with 3 cells of biome stone) and accept ~1 in 50 levels regenerating — log seeds that fail for tuning.
- Sim nondeterminism (randomized row scan direction) means restored levels are bit-exact but future-divergent — fine for terrain, but liquids at rest near a snapshot boundary can re-settle visibly on reload. The 30-tick settle pass behind the fade hides this; do not promise replay-identical worlds, only persistent ones.
- Backtracking tedium is the genre's classic failure: if shortcut wells slip out of Wave 4, re-ascending five levels on a 1.6s levitation budget will read as punishment. The steam updraft on cleared wells (Wave 1) is the insurance policy — ship it early and tune it generously.
- Sensor and emitter creep: each is cheap (<2k cell reads per check) but they accumulate across waves; keep a hard per-level budget (6 sensors, 8 emitters) enforced by the generator, and keep them in sparse lists — never add another full-grid sweep to the sim tick.
- Branch biomes double art-free content but also double tuning surface: Fungal Gloom's Spore Gas chain-detonations in confined CA caves can trivially one-shot players. Every new flammable/explosive cell needs a confined-space damage rehearsal in the existing BUILD mode before it ships in a level.
- Cell enum values are save-format ABI now: snapshots in IndexedDB outlive code versions. Append-only on the Cell table, version-stamp snapshots, and write the migration-or-discard policy in Wave 1, not when the first player loses a save.


# DESIGN 2

## vision
You are a wandsmith spelunker descending through a world made entirely of simulated matter. Every weapon you will ever hold is a sentence you wrote yourself — wand frames are grammar (cast delay, mana, capacity), spell cards are words (projectiles, modifiers, triggers), and the world is the reader: oil you trailed last fight ignites this one, the puddle an enemy bled into becomes a lightning conduit, the lake a boss hides in can be frozen into your floor. What makes it distinct from Noita is restraint fitted to web scale: a deterministic no-shuffle cast compiler you can read at a glance, ~27 cards that are all compositions of primitives the engine already has (explosion, material paint, ray, arc, particle, warp, implosion), and a status system whose source of truth is not a buff table but the literal cells touching your body. Combat is chemistry with a trigger.

## coreLoop
MINUTE-TO-MINUTE: read the room as materials (oil pool, water channel, sand ceiling, metal vein) -> open the fight by spending the environment (ignite, electrify, freeze, collapse) -> kite with movement + pogo-pick while the wand cycles through its card deck -> finish stragglers with melee, vacuum the physical gold cells off the floor -> loot 0-2 cards, refill the potion bottle from a pool. EVERY 2-4 FIGHTS: a calm moment (no enemy within 120 cells) unlocks wand editing — slot new cards, swap between your 2 carried wands, agonize over the 6-card backpack. PER BIOME: find 1-2 shrines (fuse cards, drink a mana spring dry, gamble at the chaos altar), spend gold at the shop idol, then the boss gate — a sim-arena fight whose cheese strategies are alchemy, not DPS. RUN-TO-RUN: new wand frame rolls and card offerings force different sentence structures (stream-channel run, mortar run, trigger-combo run); the real metaprogression is player knowledge of card x material x status interactions, Noita-style — plus an unlocked-card codex that seeds future drop pools.

## features

### Wand Frame System [combat, L]
A wand = frame stats + ordered card slots. Frame stats: capacity (2-8 slots), castDelay (0-24 frames between casts), rechargeTime (0-90 frames after the deck wraps), manaMax (60-240), manaRegen (1.0-2.6/frame), spreadDeg (0-12), plus 0-1 innate trait. Six archetypes seed procedural generation: Apprentice (cap 3, delay 14, recharge 30, mana 100/1.4, spread 2 — the un-droppable starter), Rapid Rod (cap 4, delay 5, recharge 45, mana 80/1.8, spread 6), Heavy Staff (cap 5, delay 24, recharge 70, mana 160/1.2, spread 0, innate +25% projectile damage), Geyser (cap 6, delay 10, recharge 55, mana 120/1.6, spread 10, innate: shuffles deck each cycle but -20% all mana costs), Channel Focus (cap 2, delay 0, recharge 0, mana 140/2.4 — built for stream cards), Twin Catalyst (cap 4, delay 12, recharge 40, mana 110/1.5, innate: every projectile duplicated at +8 deg, 65% damage). Procedural drops: tier T (1-5 by biome depth) gets stat budget 100+40T spent across the stat ranges, guaranteed +1 capacity per 2 tiers. Player carries 2 wands (Q swaps), edits only when no enemy is within 120 cells. Replaces SPELL_PARAMS/SpellId dispatch in src/config/params.ts and SpellsApi.firePlayerSpell in src/core/types.ts; PlayerState.spell/cooldown become wandIndex + per-wand deck pointer.
SIM: Wand frames are physical pickups rendered as 3x9 procedural pixel sprites lying in the world; they float on liquids by density and are flung by shockwaves like any FlyingParticle. The starter Apprentice wand cannot be destroyed; dropped wands CAN be dissolved by acid or burned (oil-soaked frames), so the world threatens your loot.

### Cast Compiler (deck evaluation) [combat, XL]
Deterministic left-to-right evaluation, no shuffle by default. On fire input with cooldown 0: walk slots from the deck pointer; MODIFIER cards push onto a mod-stack and continue; MULTICAST(n) sets draws=n; each PROJECTILE card consumes a draw (default draws=1) and collects until draws are satisfied or deck end. Sum mana; if mana < cost, the cast waits (no partial casts — readable). Spawn collected projectiles simultaneously with the merged mod payload, clear mod-stack, advance pointer; pointer past end -> rechargeTime then reset, else castDelay (+ per-card castDelayAdd). TRIGGER cards spawn a carrier projectile whose payload = the NEXT collected block, cast at impact/timer; payload depth hard-capped at 1 (a payload may not contain another trigger) to kill recursion. Projectile struct (src/core/types.ts) gains: dmg, dmgType, gravity, bounces, pierceCells, trailMaterial+trailRate, homing, payload?: CardId[], modBits. Stream cards (Flame Jet, Water Jet, Excavate) drain mana/frame while held and ignore castDelay only on Channel-trait frames. Caps: 64 live projectiles, merged damage multiplier clamped at x4, mana cost floor 1.
SIM: Every spawn function is a composition of existing engine primitives: explosion.trigger(r), world.types disc/ray paints, lightning.cast, particles.spawn with material re-deposit, digRay/erodeAt, executeWarp, negative-strength shockwaves. No new sim verbs are invented — the compiler only sequences them.

### Spell Card Set (27 cards, 4 kinds) [combat, XL]
PROJECTILES (mana / damage / speed / notes): 1 Spark Bolt 6/9 kinetic/v13/life 60, r=3 erode puff on hit. 2 Bouncing Ember 9/8 fire/v9/life 180, 4 bounces at 0.75 restitution, deposits 3 Ember cells per bounce. 3 Heavy Slug 16/26 kinetic/v18, pierces 6 cells of soft material (erodes a tunnel), knockback 3.0. 4 Bomb 25, fuse 90, explosion r=26, lob gravity 0.25. 5 Mega Bomb 60, fuse 120, r=52 (current bomb numbers, now a rare card). 6 Chain Lightning 24/22 electric, range 300, 2 branches, injects world.charge into conductors. 7 Flame Jet stream 1.2 mana/frame, heat 20. 8 Excavate Ray stream 1.0/frame, erode r=2, range 160. 9 Water Jet stream 0.9/frame: sprays Water cells in an 8 deg cone v11, 0 damage, push force 0.6, douses fire, applies WET — the utility stream. 10 Acid Flask 18, lob grav 0.3, shatters into 70 Acid cells r=7. 11 Frost Shard 12/12 frost/v14, on hit r=9: water->ice, lava->stone, acid->slime; +35 FROZEN buildup. 12 Warp Bolt 28/v12 (existing warp). 13 Black Hole 55, charge-up vortex to r=140, implosion shockwave (existing, capstone rarity). 14 Gunpowder Burst 14: shotgun of 7 live Gunpowder-cell particles, 18 deg spread, 5 kinetic each — misses seed the floor with explosive grains. 15 Nitrogen Globe 20, lob, bursts into 50 Nitrogen cells r=6, +60 FROZEN in r=12. MODIFIERS (apply to next projectile): 16 Damage Plus +8 flat, +4 mana, +2 castDelay. 17 Speed Up vel x1.6, dmg x1.15, spread -2, +2 mana. 18 Sniper Sight spread=0, homing (steer 0.12 rad/frame toward nearest enemy within 90 cells), +9 mana. 19 Oil Coat: trails 1 Oil cell per 2 frames, applies 50 OILED on hit, +5 mana. 20 Bounce: +3 bounces, +90 life, +4 mana. 21 Drill Tip: erode r=2 tunnel, pierce 14 cells (not Metal), +8 mana. 22 Electrify: on hit inject charge=6 into 5x5, +10 electric dmg vs WET, +7 mana. 23 Mortar: gravity 0.35, dmg x1.4, +3 mana. MULTICASTS: 24 Twin Cast (next 2 simultaneous, +6 deg, total mana x1.1). 25 Triple Cast (next 3, +12 deg, x1.15). TRIGGERS: 26 Impact Trigger (carrier = spark-bolt stats, 12 mana + payload) — enables bomb-warp, frost-then-shatter, blackhole delivery. 27 Timer Trigger (payload casts after 30 frames mid-flight). Rarity tiers 1-4 gate drop depth; the 7 legacy spells survive as cards so day-one saves still feel familiar.
SIM: 11 of 27 cards write materials directly into the grid (oil/acid/water/nitrogen/ember/gunpowder paints, ice transmutation), 3 manipulate charge, 3 erode terrain, 2 use shockwave push/pull. Card combos ARE alchemy: Oil Coat + Bouncing Ember turns any corridor into a firetrap; Water Jet + Electrify is a homemade taser; Drill Tip + Impact Trigger + Bomb is shaped demolition.

### Sim-Driven Status System [alchemy, M]
Five statuses on ALL entities (player included), each a 0-100 meter. Source of truth is the grid: every 4 frames sample 12 cells in the entity AABB and bucket by material. WET (3+ water cells -> 100, decay 0.4/frame): extinguishes/immunizes BURNING, +50% electric damage taken, FROZEN buildup x2, -15% accel. OILED (oil contact -> 100, decay 0.15 — sticky): any fire contact ignites a 240-frame burn instead of 90, friction -25% (you slide), drips Oil cells while above 50. BURNING (fire/lava/ember contact unless WET): 1 dmg per 12 frames, duration 90 (240 oiled); the entity EMITS 1 Fire cell per 8 frames into the grid — burning enemies are mobile arsonists. FROZEN (buildup from nitrogen/ice/frost hits; at 100): enemies freeze solid 80 frames, take x2 kinetic/blast, and shatter-die to any hit >= 20 kinetic (gore burst of Ice cells); player instead gets CHILLED, -50% move for 40 frames. ELECTRIFIED (standing on charge>0 cells or electric hit): 8 dmg + 20-frame stun; if WET, arcs to every entity within 40 cells sharing the connected liquid body (flood-fill capped at 200 cells, reusing the chain-lightning conductor walk). Damage types kinetic/fire/electric/corrosive/frost/blast with a per-EnemyDef resist multiplier map. Floating damage numbers in a procedural 3x5 pixel font, 10% crit at x1.6, status glyphs as 3x3 pixel icons above heads.
SIM: This is the keystone: statuses are READ from cells and WRITE cells back (burning emits fire, oiled drips oil), so entity state and world state form one feedback loop. Zero bookkeeping desync possible — if the water drains away mid-fight, you are simply no longer wet.

### Melee: Alchemist's Pick + Pogo Kick [combat, M]
Always-equipped pick (F / right-click): 3-swing chain 8/8/14 kinetic, 18-frame swings, arc radius 12 in facing direction, no mana. The arc DIGS soft materials (sand, gold powder, wood, gunpowder — not wall/stone/metal), so melee is also mining. Deflect: hostile FlyingParticles inside the arc get velocity reversed at x1.2 — a skill parry vs Powder Mage volleys. Hitting a FROZEN enemy always shatters. Pogo Kick (down+melee airborne): 6 dmg, bounces the player at vy=-4.5 off enemies AND off liquid surfaces (splashing 8 cells of that liquid outward), enabling Hollow-Knight-style pogo routes across lava if you dare. Guarantees no wand build can brick a run.
SIM: Pick swings displace real cells (dug material becomes ballistic debris particles that re-deposit), pogo splashes are real liquid cells that can land on enemies (splash lava on a slime = ignite it), and deflected powder-mage sand returns to the grid where it lands.

### Throwable Potion Bottle [alchemy, M]
One carried bottle (key E to throw, lob arc grav 0.3, 80 cells of contents splash on shatter r=8). Refill by standing in any liquid pool for 60 frames — the bottle takes whatever the pool is (water, oil, acid, nitrogen, lava if you have the Tongs trinket, slime, blood). Bottles found in world chests come pre-filled, weighted by biome (nitrogen in Frozen, oil in Timber). Thrown bottle is a physical projectile: it can be shot mid-air to detonate early.
SIM: The purest sim weapon: it is literally a bucket of cells. Nitrogen bottle = save-my-life freeze nova; oil bottle + any fire card = 240-frame burns; water bottle = self-extinguish or electrocution primer. Refilling DRAINS the source pool cell-by-cell (60 cells removed), so resources are spatial and finite.

### Enemy Roster Expansion (10 new, 2 new movement brains) [combat, XL]
Built on the existing EnemyDef pattern with two new movers (swimmer, burrower) plus turret. 1 ACID SLIME hp 30 bounty 12: drips 1 Acid cell/12 frames while moving, death = 80-cell acid splash r=8; it melts floors under itself and falls into its own pits. 2 FROST WISP hp 18 b14, flier: 100-frame pulse r=14 freezes liquids and adds 25 FROZEN to the player — double-edged, it builds you ice platforms. 3 POWDER MAGE hp 40 b25: telekinetically lifts a 5x3 clump of nearby Sand/Gold/Gunpowder (erases cells, spawns homing hostile particles dmg 6 that re-deposit on miss); digs r=2 for ammo if none nearby — it throws the level at you. 4 TAR BOMBER hp 22 b16, flier: lobs 30-cell oil flasks to apply OILED; lethal only in company. 5 SPARK EEL hp 26 b18, swimmer (2.2 in liquid, 0.3 flop on land): every 90 frames floods charge=6 through its connected liquid body (cap 300 cells) — get out of the pool. 6 EMBER MOTH hp 8 b5, fast flier v1.8 in swarms of 4-6, trails Ember cells; trivial alone, arson in aggregate. 7 STONE MAW hp 70 b35, burrower: erodes a 5-cell tunnel as it moves through sand/stone/wall, lunges from below after a 40-frame rumble telegraph (particles + 0.01 screenshake); its tunnels persist as traversal. 8 GLASS CANNONEER hp 14 b20: fires Heavy Slug (the player card, identical numbers) every 130 frames after a 30-frame laser-sight telegraph (DigBeam visual reuse); frost-immune, fire x2, shatters into 40 Ice cells. 9 RUST KNIGHT hp 90 b40, conductor body: 120-degree front shield immune to kinetic, slam attack r=10 blast for 14; any electric source while it stands in liquid chains for x3 — the use-the-sim teaching enemy. 10 BLOODVINE HUSK hp 35 b22, wall turret: grows real Vines cells toward you (3 cells/30 frames, max 60) that root you 30 frames on touch; vines burn (flammability 0.45 already in MATERIAL_PARAMS) to sever instantly; its death blood-splash heals nearby slimes +10. ELITE AFFIXES (8% spawn): Burning (fire trail, fire-immune), Charged (charge=4 aura 5x5), Slick (oil trail), Dense (+60% hp, +1 bounty tier) — one line of code each on top of the status engine.
SIM: Every enemy either reads the grid (eel needs liquid, maw needs diggable rock, powder mage needs granular ammo) or writes it (acid/oil/ember/vine trails, ice shatter gore). Burrower and turret AI clamp to world.simBounds so off-camera enemies never mutate frozen sim regions.

### Boss 1: The Dross Queen (Flooded biome) [combat, L]
hp 600, a 24x20 slime mass: core entity wearing a skirt of ~250 Slime cells re-painted around it each frame (fallback: sprite + smaller cell skirt). Arena: a 220-cell-wide bowl half-filled with water. P1 (100-66%): triple-hop with landing shockwaves (strength 8) that knock you into the water, volleys of 3 acid flasks. P2 (66-33%): submerges; converts the lake water->acid at 5 cells/frame (a rising chemical clock); spawns 3 Acid Slimes; you survive on ledges, or FREEZE the surface with Frost Shard (acid->slime platforms), or excavate a drain channel. P3 (<33%): core exposed for 240 frames after each hop cycle, x2 damage window. She is permanently WET: electric builds melt her. Death: the entire remaining acid lake transmutes to Gold cells (the existing acid->gold rule as a ~2000-cell payoff shower you physically vacuum).
SIM: The arena IS the boss timer (acid conversion), every counterplay is a material verb (freeze, drain, electrify), and the reward reuses the acid-to-gold transmutation rule the sandbox already ships.

### Boss 2: Kiln Colossus (Scorched biome) [combat, L]
hp 900, 16x30 golem with a Metal core (explosions cannot strip Metal — chip damage only until armor breaks). Arena: stone platform over a lava lake, 4 Metal pylons, and a water reservoir sealed behind a breakable Wood plug at the ceiling. Moves: lava-slam (raises 6-tall lava waves traveling both directions along the floor), ember meteor volley (8 hostile ember particles, grav 0.2), charge attack that erodes an r=8 path through stone — it destroys your own cover and opens lava holes. Two sim solutions: shoot the wood plug -> water floods down -> water+lava=stone builds bridges AND thermal-shocks him (80 dmg, armor stripped for 480 frames, kinetic x2); or land Chain Lightning across two pylons while he stands between them -> 60-frame ELECTRIFIED stun. Death: 300-particle ember+gold eruption.
SIM: Built entirely on existing reactions: water+lava=stone, conductor pylons via world.charge, explosion's Metal immunity, erode rays. The fight is a physics puzzle wearing a health bar.

### Boss 3: The Hollow Choir (Frozen biome) [combat, L]
Three frost-wisp sisters, hp 220 each, sharing an anger link (each death gives survivors +15% speed and -10 frames between attacks). Arena: ice cathedral whose floor is brittle Ice over a Nitrogen pool. Cycle: freeze-beam (a ray that paints Ice along surfaces toward you, cutting off routes), shatter-scream (shockwave strength 10 that converts ALL Ice within r=60 into falling ice debris — including your floor), and resurrection: a dead sister revives after 600 frames unless her dropped ice-heart (3x3 Ice block with a Metal pip) is melted with fire/lava within that window. The puzzle: you need sustained fire in a biome that punishes liquids — bring Oil Coat, Flame Jet, or lure hearts over the two lava braziers in the arena corners.
SIM: Hearts are real Ice cells melted by real fire rules; the scream uses the existing shockwave system on grid material; the beam paints Ice you can pogo or shatter. The floor's destructibility is just the sim doing its job.

### Card Economy: drops, wave chests, shop idol [progression, M]
Enemies drop cards at 6% (elites 14%, weighted to tier = biome depth +/- 1). Wave/room-clear chest: choice-of-3 cards (pick 1, others vanish — Dead Cells style decisive looting). Bosses: 1 guaranteed wand (tier+1) plus choice-of-3 rare cards. Gold = existing bounties + physically vacuuming Gold cells within r=10 (1 gold per 3 cells). Shop idol between biomes: 3 cards (40-180 gold by tier), 1 wand (220-500), reroll stock 30 gold (+15 per reroll), potion pre-fill 25. Backpack holds 6 unslotted cards; selling at the idol returns 50% value. Unlocked-card codex persists across runs and seeds drop pools (knowledge + collection metaprogression, no power creep).
SIM: Gold remains a physical material: bounty gold sprays into the world as cells, can wash away in water, sink in pits, or be melted into the boss's lava if you are careless. Loot is never a UI abstraction until it is in your pack.

### Shrines (exploration rewards) [exploration, M]
1-2 per biome, placed in side-pockets off the critical path (coordinates with the map system). ANVIL OF FUSION: sacrifice any 2 cards -> 1 random card of max(tiers)+1; the anvil glows and sprays ember cells. MANA SPRING: a pool of ~150 glowing water-variant cells; standing in it grants +20 max mana permanently while the pool visibly drains cell-by-cell — first-come, fully diegetic. CHAOS ALTAR: rerolls your held wand's frame stats within +/-30% of its budget (gamble), then ignites itself either way. Shrines are findable by their light leaking through cracks (the dynamic lighting already supports this).
SIM: Springs are consumable cell pools; the altar's self-immolation can spread to nearby wood; an anvil placed near oil is a player-made hazard. Shrines obey, never bypass, the sim.

### Wand & Deck UI [feel, M]
Tab opens the wand bench (only functional when no enemy within 120 cells; otherwise shows red-locked): two wand columns with drag-and-drop card slots, a stat panel computing effective DPS-cycle (total deck mana / regen, full-cycle time = sum castDelays + recharge), and a live dry-fire preview that casts into a 64x48 simulated thumbnail (a tiny World instance) so players SEE the cast pattern before equipping. Cards render as 12x16 procedural pixel icons generated from their primitive composition (projectile glyph + material tint + modifier border). Number keys still hot-swap nothing — all complexity lives in the bench, keeping moment-to-moment controls at move/jump/fire/melee/potion/swap.
SIM: The dry-fire preview runs the REAL cast compiler against a pocket sim world — zero divergence between tooltip and battlefield, and it is cheap (3072 cells).

## risks
- Performance: status AABB sampling, projectile material trails, and 10 enemy types stacking on the fixed-step cell sim — mitigate with the 4-frame sample cadence, 64-projectile and 200-cell flood-fill caps, per-frame trail emission budgets, and keeping all enemy world-writes inside world.simBounds.
- Cast compiler complexity creep (Noita's evaluator is a famous rabbit hole): the depth-1 trigger payload cap, no-shuffle default, and wait-on-insufficient-mana rule are load-bearing simplifications — defend them against feature requests until everything else ships.
- Balance combinatorics of 27 cards x 6 frame archetypes: enforce the global x4 merged-damage clamp and mana floor 1 in the compiler itself, not per-card, so no future card combination escapes them.
- Wand bricking and loot griefing: the indestructible starter wand plus zero-mana melee are the safety floor; destructible dropped wands (acid/fire) is flavorful but needs a 90-frame immunity after drop or boss rewards will melt in their own death lava.
- Dross Queen's live cell-skirt rendering is novel tech with unknown cost at 250 repainted cells/frame — prototype in week one of wave 4 and fall back to sprite-plus-small-skirt without changing the fight design.
- Swimmer/burrower brains interacting with the camera-bounded sim: an off-screen Stone Maw must suspend tunneling or it will desync against frozen cells — clamp all AI grid mutation to simBounds and freeze their timers outside it.
- Coordination risk with the parallel map/biome work: shrine placement, boss arena stamps, and boss-gated exits all assume the new level system exposes 'side pocket' and 'exit gate' hooks — agree on those two interfaces before wave 3 starts.
- The legacy 4120-line noita-sandbox.html still owns spells/enemies/player while src/ holds only the extracted core — wave 1 must land on the module side (extending SpellsApi/EnemyControlApi in src/core/types.ts), which means finishing the port of spell and enemy control out of the HTML before new combat code is written, or the refactor debt doubles.


# DESIGN 3

## vision
You are a scavenger-alchemist descending a physically simulated underworld where every material is simultaneously loot, weapon, tool, and key. Nothing is abstract: gold is heavy cells that lava can melt and mercury can eat, potions are real liquids you vacuum into glass flasks, brewing means literally pouring cells into a cauldron and lighting a fire under it, and progression is measured in what you can dig through, what you can bottle, and what you KNOW (recipes persist between runs like Noita's knowledge; unlock pools persist like Dead Cells). What makes it distinct from its references: the falling-sand grid is the single source of truth for the entire economy — shops sell jars of cells, shrines drink cells, wand mods spray cells — so every system feeds emergence back into the one simulation that is the game's identity.

## coreLoop
MINUTE-TO-MINUTE: descend a biome deck → spot a treasure vault shimmering behind translucent glass or matte obsidian (hardness tells you if you can crack it yet) → bottle what the biome offers (suck 8 cells/tick into a flask: acid, nitrogen, honey, spores) → solve traversal with materials (pour water on lava to bridge, throw a nitrogen flask to freeze a waterfall climbable, drain a flooded shaft with void salt) → fight nests by weaponizing terrain (ignite the toxic-gas pocket, dust-explode the spore cloud, honey-trap the golem, drop your bottled lava) → harvest gold/crystal/slag → reach the sealed exit shaft. BETWEEN DECKS (rest stop): light the cauldron, pour collected reagents, brew or mis-brew (failure = toxic sludge), drink or stash potions, spend gold at shop/tinker bench, offer 200 cells of the demanded material at the shrine, descend — the way back seals. RUN-TO-RUN: death converts 25% of carried gold to Essence; the Grimoire (recipes), Material Codex (first-discovery bonuses), and Workshop unlocks (starting flasks, spell pool, satchel loadouts) persist; raw stats never do, so stakes stay intact while every run starts smarter and with more options.

## features

### Data-Driven Reaction Matrix Core [alchemy, M]
Replace hardcoded neighbor checks scattered across handlers with a flat 34x34 lookup table: REACTIONS[a*34+b] -> {probPerTick, aBecomes, bBecomes, spawnParticle, charge, sfxKey} | null. Port all ~25 existing rules (water+lava=stone+steam, nitrogen freeze, acid corrode, fire ignition) into it first, with a debug overlay listing active reactions under the cursor. Also add a HARDNESS[cellType] table (0=powder, 1=wood/ice, 2=stone/wall/glass, 3=obsidian, 4=metal/void-salt) consulted by excavate ray and explosions, replacing the scattered METAL special-cases.
SIM: Each material handler's neighbor scan does one table lookup per neighbor (4 checks/cell, same cost as today's if-chains in src/sim handlers). Table is ~1.2k entries, fits in cache; per-tick probabilities preserve current 60fps behavior exactly. This is the substrate every other feature writes into.

### 12 New Materials (IDs 21-32 in CellType.ts) [alchemy, L]
MERCURY(21): liquid, density 9 (sinks under everything), best conductor (spreads charge 6 vs 4), acid/fire-immune, boils to Toxic Gas next to lava 8%/tick, eats adjacent Gold cells 1%/tick, nitrogen freezes it into Metal-with-life-400 that re-melts (temporary bridges!), 1 poison stack/30 ticks on player contact. GLASS(22): solid, rendered 40% translucent; forms from sand+lava 6%/tick, sand+fire 1.5%, instant in r=3 at lightning terrain impacts (fulgurites); acid-immune; explosions shatter it into shard particles (4 dmg). SPORES(23): drifting powder; fire contact = dust explosion r=3 chaining through clouds (cap 6 triggers/tick, overflow becomes Fire); +water on solid = 10%/tick germinates fungal Vines; slimes eat them and grow. HONEY(24): flowRate 0.04, entities at 45% speed and no jump, player heals 1HP/30 ticks submerged; water dilutes it 2%/tick; burns slow (life 600) leaving 20% Slag; +50% potion duration as brew sweetener. VOID SALT(25): powder that annihilates ANY adjacent liquid 100% (both->Empty + violet spark + charge 3), gases 25%/tick, extinguishes fire; only black holes destroy it; drains 5 mana/cell on touch; the portable 'delete liquid' puzzle key. SLAG(26): heavy inert powder from lava+water (split 70% stone/30% slag) and honey burn; re-melts to Lava next to lava 4%/tick (renewable forge fuel); acid dissolves it 5%/tick leaving Gold 8% (ore refining); sells 1g/12 cells. TOXIC SLUDGE(27): from acid+water interdiffusion 3%/tick (nerfs acid as terrain-delete), burns into Toxic Gas, nitrogen purifies to water, vines that drink it become spore-emitters; poison stacks (1 dmg/45 ticks, max 5). TOXIC GAS(28): life 300-600, pools under ceilings; fire/ember/charge contact = flash burn propagating through cloud (gas-pocket booms); condenses to sludge on ice. CRYSTAL(29): glowing solid (bloom 1.8, light radius 26); grows into adjacent water 0.2%/tick; passing charge 20% emits nova (charge 6 to 8 neighbors) — Tesla conduits; mined cells become homing +6-mana motes, 15% drop shard reagent. ASH(30): light powder; wood/vines finishing burn leave 40% ash; smothers adjacent fire 30%/tick; ash+water 4%/tick -> Slime (fertilizer, vines +30 energy); Stone-Skin reagent. BRIMSTONE(31): yellow powder in worldgen veins near lava; fire/charge contact -> Fire life 40 + 2 Toxic Gas + 30% r=2 pop; cauldron-crafts gunpowder (1 brimstone : 2 ash). OBSIDIAN(32): from lava+nitrogen (lava->obsidian, nitro->steam); hardness 3 — acid-immune, shrugs explosions r<12, excavate digs at 25% speed without Tier-2 dig upgrade; wraps deep treasure vaults.
SIM: All 12 are ordinary cells in the existing Uint8Array types channel with entries in MATERIAL_PARAMS, COLOR_FN, and the reaction matrix; mercury/honey/sludge reuse the liquid skeleton, spores/salt/slag/ash/brimstone the powder skeleton, gas reuses handleGas, glass/crystal/obsidian are solids in isSolid/blocksEntity. Only new render cost is a translucency flag-table branch in the pixel fill loop for glass.

### Flask System (carryable bottles) [alchemy, M]
3 flask slots at run start (buyable to 5: 80g, 200g). Flask = {materialId, amount 0-600, potionId?}. Q cycles, R toggles suck/pour at aim point: suck vacuums matching liquid/powder cells in r=4 disc at 8 cells/tick off the grid; pour emits 6 cells/tick. F throws: projectile with bomb gravity 0.14, shatters on impact into contents in r=7 splash plus a ring of 8 glass shard particles; an EMPTY thrown flask deposits 8 Glass cells (vitrify a floor with empties). Hold E 0.5s to drink (potion effect, or raw-liquid effects: water cleans poison stacks, honey heals 8, sludge poisons you). 16 of 33 materials are bottleable (all liquids + sand, gunpowder, void salt, spores, ash, brimstone, slag).
SIM: Sucked cells are literally removed from World.types (conservation: you cannot dupe matter); poured/thrown cells are real cells instantly subject to the CA — thrown nitrogen freezes, thrown acid eats, poured oil + your own flamethrower = area denial. The flask is the bridge between the 'small inventory model' and the grid: inventory stores (id, count), the grid does everything else.

### Cauldron Brewing [alchemy, M]
Cauldron = a 24-cell-wide metal basin prop stamped into the grid at every rest stop (portable mini version 150g). Every 30 ticks it histograms cell types inside its 24x20 interior. If a Fire/Lava heat source has sat beneath the basin floor for 300 consecutive ticks AND the histogram matches a recipe within +/-15% ratio tolerance, contents transmute into BREW cells (hidden Cell 33; life channel stores potionId, color from potion palette). Suck Brew into a flask -> flask becomes that potion. Wrong mix heated 600 ticks -> Toxic Sludge (failure is content). Lava poured INSIDE -> cauldron detonates r=10 spraying contents. Recipes: Levitation(water 100+crystal shard 1+spores 20), Stone-Skin(ash 80+stone 60+water 100), Acid Blood(blood 80+acid 80+honey 40), Fire Immunity(nitrogen 60+lava 60+slag 30), Transmutation(mercury 100+gold 20+brimstone 20), Void Draught(void salt 40+any potion), Mead(honey 80+water 80+spores 10), Gunpowder craft(brimstone 60+ash 120 -> 150 gunpowder cells pour from the spout).
SIM: Brewing is 100% expressed in the grid: you physically pour reagents (spillage is loss), physically build and protect the fire (water dripping from above ruins your brew — site selection matters), and the histogram sampler is a 480-cell scan every 30 ticks (negligible). Crucially this means enemies, explosions, and your own clumsiness interact with brewing mid-process.

### Potion Effects + Status Framework [combat, M]
Shared status system on player AND enemies: {poison(1 dmg/45t, max 5 stacks), slow, levitating, stoneskin, fireImmune, acidBlood, voidcast} as bitfield + timers on the entity struct. Effects: LEVITATION 12s zero levit-drain +40% fly speed (thrown: r=20 entities float helplessly 4s). STONE-SKIN 10s: -60% damage, knockback immune, -30% move, you sink in liquids, and liquid cells touching your hitbox petrify into a 1-cell Stone crust — walk across a lava lake leaving a bridge. ACID BLOOD 45s: your blood splatter cells become Acid; melee attackers take 8; your wounds eat the floor. FIRE IMMUNITY 25s: wade in lava as a thick liquid. TRANSMUTATION (throw only): r=12, metal/stone/slag -> 35% Gold, rest sand — expensive, pays out on big deposits. VOID DRAUGHT 30s: 0 mana costs, -20 max HP for the run. MEAD: 60% heal 30 / 30% drunk camera-wobble 300t / 10% both. Drinking honey-sweetened brews: x1.5 duration.
SIM: Every effect is defined by how it rewrites entity-vs-cell rules (stoneskin writes Stone cells, acid blood swaps the blood-splatter material id, fire immunity changes the damage check against Cell.Lava/Fire) — no effect exists outside the sim's vocabulary. Enemy statuses reuse the same code path so a thrown poison flask works on the golem.

### Material Hardness & Dig Progression [exploration, S]
Excavate ray gets a digTier stat (1 at start). Tier vs HARDNESS table: digs hardness <= tier at full speed, tier+1 at 25% speed, above not at all. Bombs crack hardness 2; only big bombs (upgraded, 90g) and black holes crack obsidian(3); metal/void-salt(4) yield only to black holes. Tier-2 dig upgrade sold at depth-3 rest stop (90g). Treasure gating becomes physical: glass vaults (see the loot, blunt force shatters it but the blast may destroy fragile loot — use chain lightning's small r=4 pop instead), obsidian vaults (come back richer or smarter — nitrogen flask poured on the lava moat MAKES obsidian, so the same knowledge that walls you also armors you).
SIM: One table consulted at the two existing destruction choke points (excavate handler, triggerExplosion disc pass) — replaces the hardcoded METAL-spared checks, so it simplifies code while creating a Metroidvania-style ability gate out of pure material properties.

### The Descent (run structure + rest stops) [world, L]
Replace one-arena-forever with a 6-deck vertical run: each deck regenerates the full 1600x1064 grid with that depth's biome profile (reuse existing CA cave gen + tunnel arteries). Find the exit shaft (lit landmark), drop in -> fade -> regen next deck. Between decks: a hand-stamped 500x300 Rest Stop pocket containing cauldron, shop, tinker bench, shrine, and a heal spring dripping 200 'blessed water' cells (1 HP per 10 cells absorbed on contact, then it is gone — heal is a finite physical resource). Descending seals the ceiling with bedrock (Noita-style commitment). Depths: 1 Earthen (gentle), 2 Timber (oil, spores, dust-explosion hazard), 3 Flooded (sludge, honey hives), 4 Frozen (nitrogen, crystal caves), 5 Scorched (brimstone fields, mercury pools, obsidian), 6 The Vault (gold, void-salt geodes, boss). Enemy HP x(1+0.35*depth), bounty x(1+0.4*depth). WaveDirector becomes an ambush director: nests + triggered swarms when you crack vaults or linger 90s.
SIM: Deck regen reuses the existing generator and clears all five channel arrays (types/colors/life/moved/charge) — cheap because there is only ever ONE live grid. Biome identity finally becomes mechanical, not cosmetic: each depth's signature materials seed its own puzzle/hazard vocabulary (sealed toxic-gas pockets in stone that flash-burn when you dig into them with a spark, spore clouds you must cross without a flame).

### Shops + Wand Tinkering Bench [progression, L]
Shop at each rest stop: 3 random spell offers (40-120g) from a pool grown by Workshop unlocks, flask slots, +10 max HP (60g, x1.5 scaling), reagent jars ('150 cells of brimstone', 30g), portable cauldron 150g, big-bomb upgrade 90g, Tier-2 dig 90g. TINKER BENCH: each spell has 3 mod slots; buy mods with gold: +1 projectile 50g, bounce 40g, size x1.5 60g, mana -30% 70g, homing 80g — and the star: TRAIL MOD (45g + you pour a flask into the bench): the spell now emits 1 cell of that bottled material per 2 ticks of flight. Spark bolt with oil trail + flamethrower = fire snakes; bomb with water trail = self-quenching demolition; excavate with nitrogen trail = dig-and-freeze. Fixed 3 slots keeps this Noita-wand-LITE, not a DAG editor.
SIM: Trail mods write real cells along projectile paths every other frame (same pattern as the existing fireball smoke trail), so player creativity composes directly with the reaction matrix — the bench effectively sells programmable material emitters, the cheapest-to-build highest-emergence feature in the design.

### Shrine Offerings (materials as currency) [progression, S]
Each rest stop shrine displays a demand ('The shrine thirsts for OIL') drawn from a depth-weighted table. Pour 200 cells of it into the shrine basin (literal grid container with a fill-level histogram check, same tech as cauldron) -> choose 1 of 2 blessings for the next deck: +15% spell damage, full flask refill of any known material, map-reveal pulse, 25% shop discount, or 'transmuting touch' (your melee-range gold harvest radius +50%). Overpour is wasted; pour the WRONG liquid and the shrine curses you (1 random flask emptied).
SIM: Reuses the cauldron's container-histogram sampler verbatim. Turns raw materials into a second currency, which makes BOTTLING WHILE EXPLORING always relevant ('do I keep this acid as a weapon, brew with it, or has the next shrine been thirsting for it?') — a Dwarf-Fortress-style economy loop with zero new sim code.

### Material Economy Rebalance (gold has physics) [progression, S]
Nerf acid->gold transmutation from 10% to 3% and make it require the acid cell to ALSO border water (dilute aqua regia fiction, prevents the infinite-money hose). Gold stays physical: mercury eats it, lava melts it (cell deleted, 'lost gold' particle), explosions scatter it as homing pickups only within 30 of you. New income: slag smelting (1g/12 cells at shop), crystal motes, vault hoards (200-600 gold cells), first-discovery Codex bonuses (10 Essence). Target economy: depth clears yield ~150-250g, total run income ~1200g vs ~1500g of desirable sinks, so every shop visit is a real choice.
SIM: All money exists as Gold cells or homing particles already supported by runHarvesterField; the rebalance is pure reaction-table and constant tuning. Money you have not banked can burn — carrying wealth through the Scorched Wastes is itself a risk decision.

### Grimoire + Material Codex (knowledge persistence) [progression, S]
Grimoire: recipes are unknown until you brew them once (or buy a recipe page 50g); once discovered, persisted forever in localStorage and shown as a ratio card at any cauldron. Codex: first time you ever CAUSE each reaction pair (mercury+nitrogen, spores+fire...) it is recorded with a 10-Essence bounty — turns the 34x34 matrix itself into a collectible exploration space (~60 notable entries curated, not all 1156). In-run UI: hovering a material with the cursor shows known reactions only.
SIM: The reaction-matrix execution path emits a typed event (existing event bus) with (a,b) pair ids when player-caused; Codex just subscribes. Discovery-as-progression mirrors Noita's strongest meta idea and costs almost nothing on top of the matrix core.

### Workshop (between-run meta hub) [progression, M]
On death, 25% of carried gold converts to Essence (plus Codex bounties). Workshop screen (not a level) spends Essence on OPTION unlocks only, never stats: starting flask count 1->2 (50E) ->3 (150E), new spells added to the shop pool (60-120E each), starter satchel loadouts ('Pyromancer: brimstone jar + fire immunity recipe pre-known', 100E), portable cauldron from run start (200E), shrine offers 3 choices instead of 2 (120E). Dead Cells structure, Noita stakes: a fresh player and a 50-hour player have identical max power, different breadth.
SIM: Pure inventory-model layer; its only sim contact is that every unlock ultimately resolves to materials/recipes/cells (a satchel IS a pre-filled flask of real cells). LocalStorage save with schema version field from day one.

### Worldgen Set-Pieces (vaults, geodes, hives, pockets) [world, M]
Per-deck stamped features using existing carve tech: GLASS VAULTS (loot chamber behind 3-cell glass shell, ambush trigger on shatter), OBSIDIAN VAULTS (depth 4+, need Tier-2 dig or chemistry), SALT GEODES (void salt crust around crystal cores, depth 5+), HONEY HIVES (flooded deck: honeycomb wax-wood lattice dripping honey, guarded by fast imp variants), BRIMSTONE FIELDS (scorched: yellow veins that chain-ignite — crossing them with a flamethrower equipped is a war crime against yourself), SEALED GAS POCKETS (Stone bubble containing 300-800 Toxic Gas cells at slight overpressure: digging in vents it toward you), CRYSTAL CAVES (frozen deck: light-emitting clusters + charge-nova chains that imps deliberately trigger). 2-4 set-pieces per deck, placed on the existing tunnel-artery graph so they are always findable.
SIM: Set-pieces are just cell stamps — every one is a pre-authored CHEMICAL situation rather than a scripted event, so each interacts with all player tools (you can drain the hive with void salt, freeze the gas pocket's vent with nitrogen, detonate the brimstone field from range as area denial).

- HARDNESS table replacing METAL special-cases in excavate + triggerExplosion
- First 6 materials: Glass, Honey, Toxic Sludge, Toxic Gas, Ash, Slag (IDs 21-26 ordering flexible) with full matrix entries

- Acid->gold nerf to 3%+water-adjacent (economy guardrail before flasks make acid trivially portable)
- Gas-pocket + dust-explosion safety caps (max 6 chain triggers/tick)
WHY: Highest fun-per-effort in the whole plan: flasks alone roughly double the playability of the EXISTING game (portable water vs lava, throwable acid, bottled nitrogen) with zero new level structure, and the reaction-matrix refactor is the substrate every later wave writes into — doing it first means materials 7-12, potions, shrines, and trails are all data entries instead of code. Shippable today into the current arena loop.

### Wave 2 — The Cauldron (brewing update)
- Cauldron prop + container histogram sampler + heat-under-basin detection + Brew cell (id 33, potionId in life channel)
- Status-effect framework on player and enemies (poison stacks, slow, buffs as bitfield+timers)

- Grimoire recipe persistence (localStorage, versioned schema) + recipe cards at cauldron
- Cauldron placed at arena spawn so the feature ships inside the current map
WHY: Second because it consumes Wave 1's flasks and materials and converts them from tactical tools into a crafting GAME with discovery (mis-brews, recipe hunting) — the moment the title earns 'Alchemist'. Status framework built here is required by everything later. Still needs no new world structure, so it is independently shippable and immediately fun in the arena.

### Wave 3 — The Descent (the structural update)
- 6-deck regeneration run structure with exit shafts, ceiling seal, depth scaling (HP x1+0.35d, bounty x1+0.4d)
- Rest Stop pocket gen: cauldron, heal spring (finite blessed-water cells), shrine + shop + bench placeholders
- Remaining 6 materials: Mercury, Spores, Void Salt, Crystal, Brimstone, Obsidian with matrix entries
- Biome material identities wired into src/config/biomes.ts gen profiles (brimstone veins, crystal clusters, honey hives, salt geodes)
- Worldgen set-pieces: glass vaults, obsidian vaults, sealed gas pockets, brimstone fields (2-4 per deck on tunnel arteries)
- Dig-tier gating (Tier-2 dig as a findable/buyable upgrade)
- WaveDirector repurposed as ambush director (nests + vault-crack triggers)
WHY: Third because run structure is the biggest lift and only pays off once there are materials worth bottling and brews worth carrying between decks — Waves 1-2 are its content. This is the wave that delivers the user brief's exploration/biome-diversity/map mandate: biomes become chemically distinct, descent gives the Noita-style commitment arc, and vault gating turns hardness into Metroidvania keys.

### Wave 4 — Gold Has Weight (economy update)
- Shops at rest stops (spell offers, flask slots, HP, reagent jars, portable cauldron, big bomb, dig tier)
- Wand tinker bench: 3 mod slots/spell, 5 stat mods + the flask-fed material-trail mod
- Shrine offerings with container-pour demands, blessings, and wrong-pour curses
- Slag smelting income + vault hoards + economy tuning pass (target ~1200g income vs ~1500g sinks per run)
- Transmutation and Void Draught potions (the two economy-touching brews, gated behind expensive reagents)
WHY: Fourth because sinks only matter once the descent generates real income and scarcity (Wave 3), and the trail mod — the single highest-emergence purchase — needs both flasks (W1) and shops to exist. After this wave every gold piece and every bottled cell has at least three competing uses, which is the Dwarf-Fortress economy heartbeat of the design.

### Wave 5 — The Workshop (meta update)
- Essence conversion on death (25% of gold) + Material Codex first-discovery bounties wired to reaction-matrix events
- Workshop hub screen: starting flasks, spell-pool unlocks, starter satchels, portable-cauldron start, 3-choice shrines
- Codex/Grimoire browsing UI (known reactions on material hover in-run)
- Daily seed mode + run summary screen (depth, discoveries, essence earned)
- Balance pass across the full loop with telemetry counters (deaths by cause, brews per run, shrine fulfillment rate)
WHY: Last because meta-progression is only motivating once runs are rich enough to vary (needs all four prior waves) and because its design explicitly avoids power creep — it sells breadth, so it cannot rescue a shallow core, only multiply a deep one. Ships the retention layer: knowledge + options persist, stakes survive.

## risks
- Reaction-matrix migration double-fire: porting the 21 existing hardcoded rules into the table while old handler checks remain risks reactions firing twice per tick (e.g., water+lava consumed at 2x rate) — port material-by-material with the debug overlay and golden-image sim tests on seeded scenes.
- Chain-explosion perf cliffs: spore dust explosions and toxic-gas flash-burns can cascade hundreds of triggerExplosion calls in one tick; hard-cap chained explosions at 6/tick with overflow degrading to plain Fire cells, and budget-test the worst case (full-screen spore cloud + flamethrower) on a low-end laptop.
- Channel overloading: storing potionId in the Int16 life channel for Brew cells collides with life's countdown semantics elsewhere (INVENTORY.md already flags vine sentinel overloading); if any generic decay path touches Brew cells the potion identity silently corrupts — guard with an explicit type check or move cauldron contents to a small side map keyed by region.
- Economy exploits: portable flasks + acid + the existing acid->gold rule is an infinite money printer unless the nerf (3% + water-adjacent) ships IN THE SAME WAVE as flasks; similarly Transmutation Brew must cost more in reagent market value than its expected gold yield except on large metal deposits.
- Sim-window staleness vs new mechanics: charge outside the camera window never decays today, and the cauldron's 300-tick heat timer or shrine fill state would freeze if the player walks away — rest-stop logic must either pin a small always-simulated window around the cauldron or store wall-progress timestamps, or brews will feel bugged.
- Deck regeneration must fully reset all five channel arrays plus stains, particles, projectiles, and the ambush director, and guarantee a safe spawn (entityFree above floor) — the respawn code already REASSIGNS the projectiles/enemies arrays, a known aliasing hazard for any module holding references.
- Scope creep on wand tinkering: the bench is deliberately Noita-LITE (fixed 3 slots, flat mods); resisting the pull toward full wand-building DAGs is a design discipline risk more than a technical one — the trail mod delivers 80% of the emergence at 10% of the cost.
- Translucent glass rendering adds a per-pixel branch in the hot Float32 fill loop; measure before committing, and fall back to a pre-dithered opaque palette if it costs more than ~0.5ms.
- Save persistence (Grimoire/Codex/Workshop) needs a versioned localStorage schema from Wave 2 onward or later waves will face migration bugs; cap Codex to ~60 curated reaction pairs so first-discovery events stay meaningful and testable.
- Player-griefs-self interactions (pouring bottled sand entombing the player, stone-skin sinking you into lava you just petrified, void salt deleting your own heal-spring water) are features at low frequency but need escape valves: excavate always works on hardness 0-1, and drowning/entombment damage ticks slowly enough to dig out.
